Aníbal Olivera Morales


Ph.D(c) in Social Complexity Sciences



I am a Ph.D student at the Research Center for Social Complexity (CICS), where my research focuses on social contagion and diffusion processes within networks using advanced quantitative methods. Drawing from a background in Physics (Bs. 2020, Ms. c. 2022, at UdeC), my expertise extends to various domains, including works in theoretical physics and applications in epidemiology, alongside a strong emphasis on developing Social Network Analysis tools, such as co-authoring the R package netdiffuseR and have co-led workshops on its application.

My primary interest lies in leveraging these computational and statistical approaches to illuminate complex social dynamics, a focus clearly reflected in my contributions to FONDECYT-funded projects on prison populations and political dynamics. I have also ventured into undergraduate teaching research to bring Physical Sciences closer to Engineering students.
